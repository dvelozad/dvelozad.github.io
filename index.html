About

I'm a PhD researcher in the Institut für Physik at Johannes Gutenberg University Mainz, working where statistical mechanics, molecular simulation, and high-performance computing meet.

My research focuses on adaptive resolution simulation and Monte Carlo methods — computing chemical potentials of hydrogen-bonded aqueous mixtures through Kirkwood–Buff theory, and modelling charge transport in electrochemical systems.

I care about methods that are both physically grounded and computationally honest: getting the accuracy–cost trade-off right, and writing simulation code that others can actually build on. I did my MSc in Physics at the Universidad Nacional de Colombia, Bogotá.
